This is where you manage the **caterer side** of the marketplace – who delivers, from which location, on
what terms, and what's in the catalog.

## Caterers & locations

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Retailer" icon="building-user">
    A **caterer** (marketplace partner). Has branches, products, packages and contact persons; carries
    scores and capability taxonomies that matching uses.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Branch" icon="location-dot">
    A **caterer location** (kitchen). A retailer can have multiple branches – each with opening hours,
    minimum order value, take rate and delivery area. Orders and offers hang off a branch.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Billing & payout

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="MonthlyRetailerBill" icon="file-invoice-dollar">
    The monthly **credit note** per branch / month (with PDF). Bundles payouts, fees and manual
    adjustments.
  </Card>
  <Card title="PayoutAdjustment" icon="sliders">
    Manual correction line items on a monthly bill. Gross and tax are calculated automatically from the
    net amount × tax rate.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  The monthly credit note has several payout components (including deposit and the egora voucher share).
  If individual line items and the total don't match, there's a reconcile command
  (`audit:monthly-payout-reconcile`).
</Note>

## Catalog

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Product" icon="box">
    A catalog product of a caterer. Carries variants, options, categories and allergens.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Productvariant" icon="boxes-stacked">
    The actually sellable unit of a product – this is what ends up in offers and orders.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Package" icon="box-open">
    A bundled, priced catering package from a caterer (menu including markups for drinks, equipment,
    service, headcount).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Voucher" icon="ticket">
    Vouchers; the refund can be split between egora and the caterer.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Product categories drive the **tax rate** (via the category default rate). If you create or edit a
  category inline, make sure to pick the correct leaf category instead of the menu group.
</Tip>
