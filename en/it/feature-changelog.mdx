The core problem this knowledge base addresses: **features go live without being communicated** — to
the people they affect. This page shows a repeatable pattern, so a feature isn't considered "done"
until its user-facing change is written down for every audience.

<Note>
  The entries below are **illustrative examples** to show the format — not real release notes.
</Note>

## The pattern

Every shipped feature gets a changelog entry that answers three questions:

<Steps>
  <Step title="What changed (one sentence)">
    Plain language, no jargon — the behavior a user would notice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Who's affected">
    Tag the audiences: **KAM**, **customers**, **caterers**. If an audience is affected, the entry
    has to say what *they* should do differently.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Where to find more">
    Link to the relevant page in the customer or caterer tab so the announcement and the reference
    docs stay in sync.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Changelog

<Update label="2026-07-03" description="Lieferung/Abholung: catalog-only rule now enforced everywhere; new 'Abholung' catalog item; 19 % default">
  The "Lieferung and Abholung must come from the standard catalog" rule now applies to **every** product-creation path — not just the Filament menu editor: Filament _New Product_, Product edit (title field), Nova and Filament product resource, the _New Product_ modals in **Catering Request Detail** and **Catering Offer Detail**, the AI menu-import pipeline, and the Nova action **"Convert caterer proposal to catering offer"**. Matching is case-insensitive and recognises common title stems (_liefer_, _versand_, _delivery_, _logistik_, _anfahrt_ → **Lieferung**; _abhol_, _pickup_, _rücktransport_ → **Abholung**; combined "Lieferung & Abholung" lines count as Lieferung). In addition:

  - **New catalog item "Abholung"** (caterer collecting / returning equipment after the event — Rücktransport, not customer self-pickup). Previously only "Lieferung" existed, so pickup fees always minted free custom products.
  - **"Lieferung" default tax rate is now 19 %** (7 % remains allowed). Brings the catalog in line with the actual fee billing.
  - **Catalog-backed products can no longer be deleted** — in Nova or Filament. Historical orders and offers reference them, and deleting one would orphan that history.
  - **DB level:** a caterer can hold only **one** product per standard-catalog item (unique index). Existing duplicates were consolidated beforehand via `audit:delivery-pickup-products`.

  <CardGroup cols={3}>
    <Card title="KAM" icon="headset">
      If a caterer reports that they can no longer create "Lieferung", "Abholung", "Versand" or "Rücktransport" as a standalone product: that's intended — they need to pick the entry from the **standard product catalog**. "Abholung" is newly available in the catalog, you don't need to create it. Cart tax rates for delivery can still be 7 % or 19 %; the catalog default is now 19 %.
    </Card>
    <Card title="Customers" icon="user">
      Not affected.
    </Card>
    <Card title="Caterers" icon="utensils">
      Delivery and return-transport lines (titles like "Lieferung", "Versand", "Abholung", "Rücktransport", "Logistikpauschale", "Anfahrtspauschale") must now be added from the **standard product catalog** **everywhere** in the backend — including single-product creation, the offer/request modal, and the AI menu import. A custom product with such a title is rejected. "Abholung" is newly available as its own catalog entry. Catalog-backed products can no longer be deleted.
    </Card>
  </CardGroup>
</Update>

<Update label="2026-06-30" description="Cart: ‘Add to catalog’ directly from the line item">
  Nova cart views (OrderProductsCard and OfferProductsCard) now show a status pill on every line
  item: **'Available in catalog'** or **'This offer only'**. For _offer-only_ products in edit mode,
  a green **'Add to catalog'** button appears next to the pill — one click clears the _offer\_only_
  flag and sets the product status to _Available_, so the caterer can reuse the product from their
  standard catalog. Packages have no offer\_only field and are unaffected. The action is admin-only.

  <CardGroup cols={3}>
    <Card title="KAM" icon="headset">
      When you spot a line item marked **'This offer only'** in an offer or order detail view that
      should live in the caterer's catalog, turn on edit mode and click **'Add to catalog'** — the
      product is promoted into the caterer's standard catalog immediately. No more detour through
      the product detail view.
    </Card>
    <Card title="Customers" icon="user">
      Not affected.
    </Card>
    <Card title="Caterers" icon="utensils">
      Not directly affected — KAM can now promote custom products into your standard catalog from
      the cart. A product previously marked 'this offer only' becomes available in your catalogs for
      future requests.
    </Card>
  </CardGroup>
</Update>

<Update label="2026-06-30" description="Cart line items: note field clearly labelled as customer-facing">

<Update label="2026-06-30" description="Offer / menu-proposal send: category and tax-rate gates before dispatch">
  Sending an offer to the customer now runs two extra checks in Nova (actions **"Send bundled
  catering offers to customer"** and **"Send catering menu proposal based on offer to customer"**):

  - **Category gate:** If any line item is missing a product category, the action is blocked and
    Nova lists the affected items. Use **"Review item categories"** on the offer detail view to fix
    them.
  - **Tax-rate gate:** When the action modal flags tax-rate issues (cart 7 % / 19 % vs the category
    or master rate), the **"I have checked the cart tax rates"** checkbox must be ticked before the
    send goes through. Clean carts show no modal element.

  <CardGroup cols={3}>
    <Card title="KAM" icon="headset">
      Before sending an offer or menu-proposal email, the category gate runs — if any line item has
      no product category, Nova rejects the action and names the items. Open the offer detail view
      and use **"Review item categories"**. When the modal shows a tax-rate notice, compare the cart
      rates against the listed master/category rates before ticking the checkbox.
    </Card>
    <Card title="Customers" icon="user">
      Not affected.
    </Card>
    <Card title="Caterers" icon="utensils">
      Not affected.
    </Card>
  </CardGroup>
</Update>

<Update label="2026-06-29" description="Multi-date orders: line-item quantities edit without service_date errors">
  In multi-date orders, editing the **quantity** of an existing line was incorrectly rejected with
  "Service date is required…" whenever that legacy line carried no `service_date`, even though the
  service date wasn't being touched. The guard now fires only when a line is created or its
  service_date is actively cleared; plain quantity updates on legacy lines go through again.

  <CardGroup cols={3}>
    <Card title="KAM" icon="headset">
      You can adjust quantities in multi-date orders directly again. If you notice a line without a
      service date, set one when you get the chance — new lines without a service date are still
      blocked.
    </Card>
    <Card title="Customers" icon="user">
      Not affected.
    </Card>
    <Card title="Caterers" icon="utensils">
      Not affected.
    </Card>
  </CardGroup>
</Update>
