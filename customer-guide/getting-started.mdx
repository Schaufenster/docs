Getting started with Egora takes just a few minutes. Once your account is set up, you can browse caterers, build your first order, and schedule delivery — all from one platform.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Activate your account">
    You'll receive an invitation email from your Key Account Manager (KAM). Click the link inside to set your password and log in to the Egora platform for the first time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your preferences">
    Add your office address, default headcount, and any dietary requirements your team has — such as vegan, gluten-free, halal, or nut-free. These preferences will be pre-applied to every order you place.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse caterers">
    Explore the catering partners available in your area. Use filters to narrow results by cuisine type, minimum order value, dietary options, or customer rating.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place your first order">
    Select a caterer, choose your menu items and quantities, confirm your delivery date and time, and submit. You'll receive an order confirmation by email straight away.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track your order">
    Log in to the platform at any time to check your order status. You'll also receive email updates as your order moves through preparation and delivery.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Account roles

Egora gives your organisation two levels of access so you can manage your team effectively.

| Role | What they can do |
|---|---|
| **Admin** | Manage billing and payment details, invite and remove team members, assign roles, access full order history, and update account settings. |
| **Member** | Browse caterers, place orders, view their own order history, and submit feedback. |

When your account is first created, your primary contact is set up as an Admin. Admins can then invite colleagues and assign them either role from the **Team** section in account settings.

<Note>
  If you haven't received your invitation email, ask your Key Account Manager to resend it. Check your spam folder first — invitation emails occasionally end up there.
</Note>
