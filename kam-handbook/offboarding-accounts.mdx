Offboarding handled well protects Egora's reputation and leaves the door open for future re-engagement. Whether a customer is leaving due to budget cuts, a strategic pivot, or a change in catering needs — or a caterer is retiring or winding down their partnership — follow this process every time. A clean exit is as important as a strong onboarding.

## Customer offboarding

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm cancellation intent and reason">
    Before taking any action, have a direct conversation with the customer to confirm they intend to cancel and understand why. Log the reason in the CRM using the standard churn reason taxonomy (e.g., budget, competitor, low usage, dissatisfaction). This data informs product and commercial decisions across the business.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Schedule an exit interview">
    Offer an exit interview — a 20-minute call — to gather more detailed feedback. Most customers will agree if you frame it as helping Egora improve. If they decline, send the written exit survey instead (template in Notion).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ensure final invoices are settled">
    Work with finance to confirm all outstanding invoices are issued and settled before archiving the account. Check for any credits or refunds owed to the customer and ensure these are processed before the account is closed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Archive the account in the platform">
    Follow the platform archiving process to deactivate the customer's account, disable login access, and prevent new orders from being placed. Do not delete the account — archiving preserves the order history for data and compliance purposes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a closing note with a re-engagement offer">
    Send a personalised closing email thanking the customer for their time on the platform, wishing them well, and leaving the door open for them to return. Include a brief re-engagement offer (e.g., a discounted first month back) if approved by your manager.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove from active Slack channels">
    Remove the customer's contacts from all active Slack channels. Archive the customer-specific channel if one exists, ensuring the channel history is preserved for internal reference.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Caterer offboarding

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the last order date">
    Agree a final order date with the caterer and document it in the CRM. This gives you a clear cut-off point to work backwards from when managing outstanding orders and customer communications.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ensure all pending orders are fulfilled or reassigned">
    Review all orders scheduled after the last order date. For each one, either confirm the caterer will still fulfil it, or reassign it to an alternative caterer and notify the affected customer immediately. No order should be left without a caterer assigned.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Settle outstanding payments">
    Coordinate with finance to confirm all payments owed to the caterer are processed before archiving. Check for any disputed invoices or unresolved credits and resolve them before the account is closed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Archive the caterer profile">
    Deactivate the caterer's profile in the platform so they no longer appear in order flows. Archive rather than delete — this preserves their order history and menu data for reference.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Notify affected customers of alternative options">
    Identify all customers who regularly ordered from the departing caterer and reach out proactively. Give them two or three alternative caterer recommendations based on their cuisine preferences and delivery location. Don't wait for them to notice the caterer has gone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Exit interview questions

Use these questions during exit interviews with departing customers. They're designed to surface actionable insight — not to relitigate the decision to leave.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What was the primary reason you decided to stop using Egora?">
    This is your most important question. Listen without interrupting or defending. The honest answer here is worth more than any other feedback you'll receive.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Was there a specific moment or incident that influenced your decision?">
    This surfaces critical incidents that may not have been formally raised as complaints. Even if the moment has passed, understanding it helps you improve the product and process.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How would you describe your overall experience with Egora's platform and service?">
    A broader satisfaction question. Useful for understanding whether the issue was operational (platform, delivery, quality) or relational (KAM support, communication, responsiveness).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Was there anything Egora could have done differently to keep your business?">
    This question often surfaces fixable issues — pricing, features, service gaps — that the customer assumed Egora wouldn't change. Take these seriously and feed them back to your manager and product team.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Would you consider using Egora again in the future, or recommending it to others?">
    Even churned customers can be advocates or return customers. A positive answer here means the door is open; a negative one tells you the relationship needs more repair before re-engagement is realistic.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there anything else you'd like to share with the Egora team?">
    Leave space for unprompted feedback. Some of the most valuable insights come from what customers say when you stop asking structured questions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Data and access

Handle all data correctly during offboarding. Getting this wrong has compliance and legal consequences.

**What gets archived:**
- Customer account record (order history, billing history, contact details)
- Caterer profile (menu history, order fulfilment records, payment history)
- All associated Gleap tickets and resolutions

**What gets deleted:**
- Active login credentials and API tokens are disabled at the point of archiving
- Personal data is retained in line with Egora's data retention policy and deleted at the scheduled retention expiry date — do not manually delete personal data outside this process

**GDPR considerations:**
If a customer or caterer contact submits a Subject Access Request (SAR) or a Right to Erasure request, do not handle this yourself. Forward the request to the Data Protection Officer immediately and log the date received. The legal response deadline is 30 days from receipt.

**Data removal requests:**
All data removal requests — whether raised during or after offboarding — must be routed to the Data Protection Officer via the designated email address. Do not action data deletion in the platform directly.

<Note>
  Tag the account as "Churned" in the CRM immediately after completing the offboarding steps. This triggers the win-back sequence in the marketing automation — which means the marketing team handles re-engagement outreach on your behalf. You don't need to do anything further unless the customer responds.
</Note>
