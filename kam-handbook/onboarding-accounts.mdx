Onboarding is the foundation of every strong account relationship. When you get it right, both your corporate customers and catering partners start with clarity and confidence — and that early trust pays dividends throughout the entire account lifecycle. Use this guide every time you bring a new account onto the platform.

## Before you begin

Make sure all prerequisites are in place before sending a single welcome email. Starting the onboarding process without these confirmed creates confusion and delays.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the signed contract is in the CRM">
    Verify the fully executed contract is uploaded and the account record is active in the CRM. If it's missing, chase the relevant signatory before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the admin contact">
    Identify and confirm the primary admin contact on both the customer and caterer side. This person will own platform access and be your main point of escalation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collect billing details">
    Ensure billing information — invoice address, PO number (if required), and payment terms — is logged in the CRM. Flag any missing fields to finance before go-live.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provision platform access">
    Create the account in the Egora platform and provision admin credentials for the relevant contacts. Confirm they have received their login details before scheduling the kick-off call.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Customer onboarding

Walk every new corporate customer through the following steps in order. Do not skip the kick-off call or the first-order walkthrough — these two touchpoints prevent the majority of early-stage support tickets.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Send the welcome email">
    Send the standard welcome email with the platform access link and a brief overview of next steps. Use the approved template in Notion and personalise the customer name, account manager name, and go-live date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Schedule the kick-off call">
    Book a 45-minute kick-off call within two business days of sending the welcome email. Use the standard agenda template in Notion, which covers platform navigation, ordering workflows, support processes, and Q&A.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Walk them through placing their first order">
    During or immediately after the kick-off call, guide the customer through placing their first order live on the platform. Screen-share if remote. Confirm they can complete the full order flow independently before you sign off.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up recurring order preferences">
    If the customer has regular meals or events (e.g., weekly office lunches), configure recurring order preferences together. Show them how to edit or cancel recurrences so they feel in control.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add to the customer Slack channel">
    Invite the admin contact to the relevant customer Slack channel for ongoing updates, announcements, and direct communication. Pin the platform access link and your contact details in the channel description.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Caterer onboarding

Caterer onboarding is equally important — a well-briefed catering partner reduces operational incidents and protects your customer accounts. Run through these steps before a caterer handles any live orders.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify menu and capacity in the platform">
    Review the caterer's menu listing for accuracy, including item names, dietary tags, allergen information, and pricing. Confirm their daily order capacity is correctly set to avoid overselling.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm delivery windows and minimum order values">
    Agree on and document delivery windows (earliest and latest delivery times) and minimum order values. Make sure these are reflected correctly in the platform settings and communicated to the caterer in writing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a test order">
    Place a test order through the platform to validate the end-to-end flow: order notification, confirmation, and fulfilment. Walk the caterer through how they'll receive and acknowledge orders.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Brief them on Gleap for support tickets">
    Show the caterer how to raise and respond to support tickets in Gleap. Cover the ticket categories they'll use most: delivery issues, order amendments, and billing queries.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add to the caterer update channel">
    Add the caterer's operations contact to the caterer update Slack channel. This is where you'll share platform updates, policy changes, and time-sensitive communications.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Handoff checklist

Before you mark an account as onboarded in the CRM, confirm every item below is complete.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Customer handoff checklist">
    1. Signed contract uploaded to CRM
    2. Admin contact confirmed and platform access active
    3. Billing details complete and validated by finance
    4. Welcome email sent and acknowledged
    5. Kick-off call completed
    6. First order placed successfully
    7. Recurring preferences configured (if applicable)
    8. Customer added to Slack channel
    9. Onboarding record updated in Notion
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Caterer handoff checklist">
    1. Menu, pricing, and allergen information verified
    2. Delivery windows and minimum order values confirmed in platform
    3. Test order completed successfully
    4. Gleap briefing completed
    5. Operations contact added to caterer update Slack channel
    6. Caterer profile marked as active in CRM
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  Use the Notion onboarding template to track progress for every new account. Link it in the account's Slack thread so the whole team has visibility — no one needs to chase you for a status update.
</Tip>
