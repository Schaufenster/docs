Every order on Egora represents a corporate client counting on you for a successful meal. Following this process ensures smooth fulfilment, happy clients, and a strong partner rating.

## Receiving an order

When a client places an order with you, it appears in the **Orders** tab of your dashboard. You'll also receive a notification via email and a platform alert at the same time — so you don't need to be logged in to know an order has come in.

Once an order arrives, you have **2 hours** to confirm it. This window exists because corporate clients often order same-day or next-day, and they need to know quickly whether their catering is confirmed.

<Warning>
  If you do not confirm an order within the 2-hour window, the platform may automatically reassign it to another catering partner. Repeated missed confirmations will affect your partner rating and order volume.
</Warning>

## Confirming an order

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the order in your dashboard">
    Go to the **Orders** tab and click on the new order to open the full order detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review items and quantities">
    Check every item and quantity carefully. Make sure you have the stock and capacity to fulfil everything exactly as ordered.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check for special instructions or dietary notes">
    Scroll to the notes section and read any special instructions from the client — these may include specific dietary requirements, delivery contact names, access instructions, or presentation preferences.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Confirm">
    If you can fulfil the order as specified, click **Confirm Order**. This notifies the client that their catering is locked in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add estimated preparation time if different from standard">
    If this order requires more preparation time than usual (e.g., a very large order or complex dietary requirements), update the estimated prep time so the fulfilment schedule stays accurate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Preparing the order

Good preparation is what separates a smooth delivery from a stressful one. Follow these best practices:

- **Label everything** — Every item, tray, or container should be clearly labelled with the customer name, item name, and any relevant dietary information (e.g., "GF", "Vegan", "Contains nuts")
- **Use insulated packaging for hot food** — Hot food must arrive at the correct temperature. Use insulated bags, boxes, or trays appropriate for the journey time
- **Build a packing checklist for large orders** — For orders with multiple items or large headcounts, create a checklist before you pack so nothing gets missed
- **Double-check against the order summary** — Before you leave, compare your packed items against the confirmed order in the platform

## Delivery

<Steps>
  <Step title="Depart with enough time to arrive at the delivery window start">
    Build in buffer time for traffic, parking, or building access. Arriving at the start of the window — not the end — sets a professional tone with the client.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark the order as 'Out for Delivery'">
    Update the order status in your dashboard before you leave. This notifies the client that their order is on the way.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deliver to the specified address and contact">
    Follow the delivery address and any access instructions in the order notes. If there's a named contact, ask for them by name on arrival.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get confirmation from the customer contact">
    Before you leave, make sure the client contact has received and acknowledged the delivery. This protects you in the event of a later dispute about delivery completion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark the order as 'Delivered' in the platform">
    Update the order status to **Delivered** as soon as the handover is complete. This triggers the payment process and closes the order on the client's side.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Handling issues during fulfilment

Sometimes things go wrong — a key ingredient doesn't arrive, a vehicle breaks down, or capacity is lower than expected. If you run into a problem that puts a confirmed order at risk, follow this process:

1. **Contact your KAM immediately** — Call or message your KAM as soon as you know there's an issue. Give them as much notice as possible so they can manage the client relationship.
2. **Do NOT cancel in the system without authorisation** — Cancelling an order yourself, without going through your KAM, causes serious disruption and triggers a client-facing failure notification.
3. **Provide a reason and any alternatives** — Let your KAM know exactly what's happened and whether you can offer a partial fulfilment, a replacement item, or a revised delivery time.

<Warning>
  Cancelling an order without contacting your KAM first is a breach of your partner agreement and may result in account suspension.
</Warning>

## After delivery

Once an order is marked as delivered, the client has the opportunity to leave a rating and feedback. Ratings are visible to your KAM and feed into your overall partner score.

If you receive a negative rating or complaint:
- Your KAM will reach out to discuss the details
- Respond promptly and constructively — provide any relevant context such as photos, packing notes, or delivery records
- Do not attempt to contact the client directly about a complaint unless your KAM advises it

A single poor review won't define your account, but a pattern of negative feedback will. Addressing complaints professionally and learning from them is the fastest way to improve your score.
