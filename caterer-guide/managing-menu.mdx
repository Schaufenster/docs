Keeping your menu accurate and up-to-date is one of the most important things you can do as an Egora partner. Incorrect menus lead to customer complaints and order issues — and both affect your partner rating.

## Adding menu items

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Menu → Add Item">
    From your dashboard, navigate to the **Menu** tab and click **Add Item** to open the item creation form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter name, description, and price">
    Use a clear, descriptive item name and write a short description that tells customers exactly what they're getting — including key ingredients, portion size, and any notable features. Set an accurate price per head or per unit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload a photo">
    Add a high-quality photo of the dish. Images should be well-lit, clearly show the food, and ideally be taken at a consistent style across your menu. Items without photos are significantly less likely to be selected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add dietary tags">
    Select all applicable dietary tags from the list (see the [Dietary tagging](#dietary-tagging) section below). Only apply tags that you can genuinely guarantee — inaccurate tagging is a serious issue for customers with allergies.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set availability">
    Choose when this item is available: every day, specific days of the week, or a defined seasonal date range. Keeping availability accurate prevents orders being placed for items you can't fulfil.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and publish">
    Click **Save** to publish the item immediately, or **Save as Draft** if you want to review it before it goes live. Published items are visible to clients straight away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Updating existing items

To edit an existing menu item, go to **Menu**, locate the item, and click **Edit**. You can update the name, description, price, photo, dietary tags, or availability at any time.

Changes take effect immediately for future orders. Confirmed orders are not affected — if a client has already ordered an item, the version they ordered stands. If a price change is significant, consider notifying your KAM so they can inform regular clients if needed.

## Managing availability

If an ingredient is out of stock or you temporarily can't offer a dish, mark it as **Unavailable** in the Menu tab. This hides the item from clients without deleting it, so you can reactivate it easily when you're ready.

<Warning>
  Do not mark an item as unavailable after an order containing that item has already been confirmed. If you discover you cannot fulfil a specific item in a confirmed order, contact your KAM immediately — do not make changes unilaterally in the system.
</Warning>

## Seasonal and rotating menus

If your menu changes with the seasons, build those changes into your schedule rather than editing reactively. Best practice is to review and update your full menu **at least quarterly** — before each season begins.

Use the **Schedule** feature (Menu → Schedule Changes) to queue menu updates ahead of time. You can set a future date for new items to go live or existing items to be retired, so the transition happens automatically without you needing to log in on the day.

This is particularly useful for:
- Summer and winter seasonal specials
- Holiday menus (e.g., Christmas, Ramadan, Diwali)
- Rotating weekly or monthly featured items

## Dietary tagging

Corporate clients frequently filter menus by dietary requirement — and they trust the tags you set. Inaccurate tags are one of the most common sources of complaints and can pose a genuine risk to customers with allergies or medical conditions.

Always tag items accurately. Here's what each available tag means on Egora:

| Tag | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Vegan** | Contains no animal products of any kind, including dairy, eggs, and honey |
| **Vegetarian** | Contains no meat or fish, but may include dairy and eggs |
| **Gluten-Free** | Contains no gluten-containing ingredients and is prepared to avoid cross-contamination |
| **Halal** | Meat is sourced from halal-certified suppliers and prepared in accordance with halal standards |
| **Nut-Free** | Contains no nuts and is prepared in an environment free from nut cross-contamination |

If you are unsure whether an item genuinely qualifies for a tag, do not apply it. It is always safer to leave a tag off than to apply one you cannot stand behind.

<Tip>
  If you regularly offer seasonal specials, ask your KAM to feature you in the monthly newsletter sent to all corporate customers.
</Tip>
