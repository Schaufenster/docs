Notion is the source of truth for Egora's KAM processes. When you update a process doc in Notion, the KAM Knowledge Base reflects those changes automatically — so KAMs always have the latest version without anyone needing to copy, paste, or republish content manually.

## How the Notion sync works

The KB connects to a designated Notion workspace using an authorized integration. Pages included in the sync must be explicitly tagged — the KB does not pull in your entire workspace. When a tagged page is updated in Notion, the sync pipeline detects the change on its next hourly run and updates the corresponding KB article in place.

Content is converted from Notion's block format into structured KB articles. Headings, body text, callouts, and tables all carry over. The KB preserves the Notion page's last-updated timestamp so KAMs can see how fresh the content is.

## Setting up the Notion connection

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the KB admin panel">
    Navigate to **Integrations → Notion** in the KB admin panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your Notion workspace">
    Click **Connect Notion Workspace**. You'll be redirected to Notion to authorize the KB integration. Grant access to the workspace that contains your KAM process documentation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select databases or pages to include">
    After authorization, choose the Notion database or top-level pages you want to make available to the sync pipeline. You can select multiple databases.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map Notion properties to KB metadata">
    Match Notion page properties to KB metadata fields — including **Title**, **Category**, and **Last Updated**. This ensures articles are correctly categorized when they arrive in the KB.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the initial sync">
    Click **Run Initial Sync** to pull all currently tagged pages into the KB. Future syncs run automatically on an hourly schedule.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tagging pages for sync

Not every page in your Notion workspace should appear in the KB. To include a page, add the **KB Sync** property to it and set the toggle to **On**. The sync pipeline checks for this property on every run and only processes pages where it is active.

You can add the **KB Sync** property to any page in a synced database directly from Notion's page view or database grid. Pages without the property — or with it toggled off — are ignored by the pipeline.

<Tip>
  Only tag pages that are ready for KAM consumption — drafts and internal planning docs should remain untagged.
</Tip>

## What gets synced

The following content syncs from Notion to the KB:

- **Page title** — used as the KB article title
- **Body content** — text blocks, headings, callouts, and tables
- **Last updated date** — pulled from Notion's built-in timestamp
- **Assigned category** — mapped from a Notion property during setup

The following content does **not** sync:

- Page comments and inline discussions
- Inline databases embedded within a page
- Linked pages (unless those linked pages are independently tagged for sync)
- Files and media attachments

<Warning>
  If you delete a page in Notion that is synced to the KB, the KB article will also be removed at the next sync cycle. Archive pages in Notion instead of deleting them to preserve the KB article while hiding the source page.
</Warning>
