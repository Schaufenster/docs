Gleap captures support messages, bug reports, and feedback directly from customers and caterers inside the Egora platform. The KB aggregates these messages into structured insights — helping you stay on top of account health and keeping help desk content relevant to what people are actually asking.

## How Gleap sync works

Gleap tickets are pulled into the KB once per day via the daily digest pipeline. When tickets arrive, the pipeline categorizes each one by type — **complaint**, **question**, **bug report**, or **feature request** — and links it to the relevant account using the customer email address attached to the ticket.

Recurring themes are tracked across the digest window. When the same issue appears across multiple tickets or accounts, the pipeline surfaces it as a KB update candidate. The KB owner reviews flagged themes and decides whether to create or update a help desk article.

## Setting up Gleap sync

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the KB admin panel">
    Navigate to **Integrations → Gleap** in the KB admin panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your Gleap project API key">
    Paste the API key from your Gleap project settings. The KB uses this key to authenticate with the Gleap API and pull ticket data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select ticket types to include">
    Choose which Gleap ticket types to pull into the KB digest — all types, or a filtered subset by type or label. If you only want customer-facing issues, exclude internal feedback labels here.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the account mapping field">
    Tell the pipeline how to link a Gleap ticket to an Egora account. Typically this is the customer email address, which maps to the corresponding account record in the KB.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the daily digest">
    Turn on **Daily Digest** to activate the scheduled pull. Digests run overnight and are available in the KB each morning.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## From Gleap to help desk

High-frequency Gleap questions don't just stay in the digest — they feed directly into help desk content. When the pipeline detects that the same question appears in five or more tickets within a rolling seven-day window, it automatically generates a candidate help desk article and flags it for the KB owner to review.

The KB owner edits and approves the candidate article before it goes live on the customer or caterer help desk. This means the help desk stays current with real support patterns, not just what the team predicted customers would ask at launch.

This loop — Gleap tickets → KB digest → help desk article — reduces repeated handling of the same questions and gives customers and caterers faster self-serve answers.

<Warning>
  Gleap data contains personal customer information. Do not share Gleap digests outside of authorized KAM channels.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Use the Gleap digest before every business review call to get a real-time picture of your account's recent support activity.
</Tip>
