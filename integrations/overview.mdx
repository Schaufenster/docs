The Egora KAM Knowledge Base doesn't require manual maintenance. It stays current by syncing with the tools your team already uses — Notion for processes, Slack for updates, Git for product changes, and Gleap for customer voice. Every time your source tools change, the KB reflects those changes automatically — so you always have accurate, up-to-date information at your fingertips.

## How sync works

Each connected source feeds into a central sync pipeline that converts raw content into structured KB articles. Here's how the flow works end-to-end:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Content is created or updated in a source tool">
    A KAM updates a process doc in Notion, a team lead posts a decision in Slack, an engineer pushes a tagged commit, or a customer submits a Gleap ticket.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The sync pipeline picks it up">
    The KB monitors each source on its configured schedule or trigger. When new or changed content is detected, the pipeline fetches and converts it into a KB-ready format.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The KB article is created or updated">
    Converted content lands in the appropriate section of the KB — categorized, tagged, and timestamped. Existing articles are updated in place; new content creates new articles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Claude Teams and the Help Desk stay current">
    Claude Teams AI draws on the latest KB content to answer KAM questions accurately. The customer and caterer help desk pulls from the same pipeline to keep FAQ pages relevant.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sync frequency

| Source | Sync Frequency | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| Notion | Hourly | Full page sync for all tagged pages |
| Slack | Real-time highlights | Only pinned messages or posts in designated channels |
| Git | On commit | Commit messages and changelogs for tagged commits only |
| Gleap | Daily digest | Categorized by account and issue type |

<Info>
  Manual overrides are possible. Ask the KB owner if you need to force a sync or exclude specific content.
</Info>
