A knowledge base is only as useful as it is accurate. This page covers the responsibilities of KB owners, the best practices for all KAMs, and the processes for flagging and fixing outdated content. When everyone follows these practices, the KB stays trustworthy — and so does the help desk and Claude assistant it powers.

## Roles and responsibilities

Keeping the KB current is a shared responsibility. Each role has a specific part to play:

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| KB Owner | Oversees all content, approves major changes, manages integrations |
| KAM | Flags outdated content, posts Slack updates, keeps Notion docs current |
| Product Team | Tags commits with `[KAM]` or `[customer-facing]` for auto-surfacing in the KB digest |
| Support Team | Logs Gleap tickets accurately to improve FAQ coverage |

## Regular maintenance checklist

Follow this cadence to keep every KB section accurate and trustworthy:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Weekly — review Gleap digest">
    Check the Gleap digest for recurring customer or caterer questions. If the same question appears more than twice in a week, update the relevant FAQ article in the KB or create a new one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monthly — audit KAM Process pages">
    Compare each KAM Process page against the current workflows documented in Notion. Flag any steps that no longer match how your team works and update the source Notion page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Quarterly — review Customer and Caterer Guides">
    Read through the Customer Guide and Caterer Guide in full. Check that every process, screenshot, and example reflects the current product. Submit a content update request or edit the Notion source directly if you have access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="On every release — check the Git commit digest">
    After each product release, review the Git commit digest for changes tagged `[customer-facing]`. For each flagged change, verify that the relevant Customer or Caterer Guide pages are updated to reflect the new behaviour before the release goes live.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to flag outdated content

If you spot something that's no longer accurate, use one of these three options:

1. **Post in #knowledge-base on Slack** — include the page name and a short description of what's wrong. The KB owner monitors this channel and will action the update or assign it to the right person.
2. **Use the "Report an issue" button** — every KB page has a "Report an issue" button at the bottom. Click it, describe the problem, and submit. This creates a tracked issue in the KB admin panel.
3. **Edit the source Notion page directly** — if you have edit access to the underlying Notion page, update it there. The next sync will pull your changes into the KB automatically.

## Content ownership

Each KB section has a designated owner who is responsible for reviewing and approving changes to that section:

| KB Section | Owner |
|---|---|
| KAM Processes | KAM Lead |
| Customer Guide | CX Manager |
| Caterer Guide | Caterer Success Manager |
| Gleap Digest | Support Team Lead |
| Git Commit Digest | Product Manager |

If you're unsure who owns a section, check the **Integrations → Content Owners** page in the KB admin panel or ask in #knowledge-base on Slack.

Owners are responsible for:
- Reviewing flagged issues in their section within two business days
- Approving or rejecting proposed changes in the manual review queue
- Proactively scheduling quarterly reviews of their section

<Tip>
  Set a calendar reminder to review your owned KB sections monthly. It takes 15 minutes and prevents KAMs from working with outdated information — which directly affects the quality of customer interactions and help desk articles.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Do not directly edit synced KB pages — changes will be overwritten at the next sync. Always update the source (Notion, Slack, etc.) first. If you're unsure whether a page is synced, check the page metadata or ask the KB owner before editing.
</Warning>
