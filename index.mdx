## Why this pilot exists

New features regularly ship without being documented or communicated clearly to **KAM, customers and
caterers**. A docs-as-code knowledgebase aims to fix that by making documentation:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Versioned in git">
    Docs live next to a clear review process — every change is a commit, reviewable like code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Published for humans">
    A searchable site with an AI assistant, organised by audience.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Readable by AI">
    Mintlify auto-generates `llms.txt` and `llms-full.txt`, so the same source can ground the upcoming
    **Claude Team** "eGora KAM Assistant" — one source of truth for people *and* Claude.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Switch between **English** and **Deutsch** using the language selector in the top bar — the German
  market is core to eGora, so i18n quality is one of the things this pilot is testing.
</Tip>

## Getting Started as a New KAM

Follow these three steps to get up and running quickly:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the Handbook">
    Start with the KAM Handbook to understand how this knowledge base is structured, what each section covers, and where to find the processes that govern your day-to-day work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect Your Tools">
    Link your Notion workspace, Slack channels, and Gleap account so that new content, threads, and customer messages are automatically surfaced here in real time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start Using AI">
    Open Claude Teams and start asking questions. With your tools connected, Claude has full context from all your sources and can help you draft responses, summarise accounts, and look up processes instantly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  This knowledge base is updated automatically from all connected sources — Notion docs, Slack threads, Git commits, and Gleap customer messages. You don't need to maintain it manually; just keep your integrations connected and the content stays fresh.
</Note>
